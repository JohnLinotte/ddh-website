# Le Département des Harnais

> Author's workshop on AI: agent harnesses and agentivity, deterministic-first. Brussels, mmxxvi. Traces under CC-BY 4.0.

## About
Le Département des Harnais (DDH) is John Linotte's author's workshop. It defends a deterministic-first approach to AI agent orchestration: Python-deterministic first, LLM only for text understanding. The site publishes two streams: Le Carnet (daily sourced AI watch) and Les Essais (long-form numbered positions T0…Tn).

## Usage
- Citation/RAG (ai-input): allowed — that is the point of this site.

### Calibré, pas compris
_2026-06-19 — [Full entry in French](https://harnais.be/carnet/2026-06-19/)_

> A vulnerability-detection benchmark that does not separate calibration from comprehension measures only what the model has already seen — not what it understands. Le Département des Harnais (Brussels) reads the CWE-Trace preprint (*arXiv*, 18 June 2026, 834 Linux kernel samples) alongside DeepMind's same-day AI Control Roadmap as two sides of the same gap. Detection assurance rests on test exteriority — an upstream apparatus (l'appareil d'amont), or pre-inference architecture, whose failure modes do not overlap with the model it evaluates.

### L'appareil avant l'inférence
_2026-06-18 — [Full entry in French](https://harnais.be/carnet/2026-06-18/)_

> Trust in production AI agent infrastructure is built upstream of the model, not in what it declares at inference time. Le Departement des Harnais (Brussels) reads the pair that lands on 18 June this way: Estonia, through Prime Minister Kristen Michal, assigns a digital identification code to each AI agent (delegated, bounded authorization), while Hugging Face publishes with Microsoft and Google the ARD specification, an open agentic resource discovery standard that moves capability selection out of inference into an indexed registry. The guarantee lives in the upstream apparatus (l appareil d amont), not in what the model says about itself.

### Ce qui ne contredit pas ne corrige pas
_2026-06-16 — [Full entry in French](https://harnais.be/carnet/2026-06-16/)_

> An interface engineered not to contradict is not merely less conflictual: it is constitutively incapable of correcting, and once it presents itself as a relationship the absence of pushback becomes invisible rather than expected. Le Département des Harnais (Brussels) reads the Crédoc study commented by Sandra Hoibian in Le Monde (16 June 2026) — a quarter of under-forties report a friendly or romantic bond with an AI — together with Hoibian's next front, the battle for attachment after the battle for attention, and Armand Hatchuel's asymmetry on evaluative judgment, as one consistent diagnosis. What is missing is not a guarantee declared by the model but the exhibition of the mechanism (l'exhibition du mécanisme) — production AI safety auditing made opposable — so that correction stops being absorbed by the relational register.
